A Trip Around the Internet



If you live in the United States, you probably already heard that the US-Canada and US-Mexico borders are closed to non-essential travel for the time being. Instead of taking a trip in real life, why not take a trip on the Internet? Well, for this special limited time offer, you can take the trip of a lifetime around the World Wide Web for the low price of $0.00! Now, before you grap your tickets at the bottom of the page, let me show you the travel plan.
Your trip will take at least 45 minutes, so make sure that you have nothing else better to do with your time. The first stop will be at a farmiliar place, known as YouTube. But this will not be your average YouTube time wasting free for all. This will be a guided YouTube tour. After we make a quick stop at the Riverside Rocks YouTube channel, (cough, cough, please subscribe), we will visit some of the finest YouTubers on the platform. We will visit, Captain Disillusion, Smarter Every Day, and Mr. Beast, all creators that put incredible ammounts of hard work into their content.
We will next visit another quite possibly farmilliar community, Glitch. (Technicly you are already here, this website is hosted with Glitch). Here you can explore the world of online programming with the very friendly community that would love to give you a helping hand.
But no trip is complete without some history. Lets venture to the first regestered domain, symbolics.com, which is an Internet museum today. Here you can learn a brief history of the Internet, starting from the very first idea to create the Internet.
For our last part of history lets venture to the Wayback Machine, the website that holds almost half a trillion archvied web pages. You can view many differnet websites from a long time ago, some up to the early 1990's. Just search the website you want to visit!
